Zackary Bowley

Data Analyst

I’ve always enjoyed running through data to solve problems, whether personal, like analyzing my Spotify to create my perfect playlist, or professionally, like working to create better programs and machines.

When I’m not working, I have a passion for the great outdoors, having spent most of my childhood exploring where I can. Most of my time is usually spent rock climbing, or outside with my coonhound, Konami, either hiking, camping or backpacking.


I have accumulated three years of hands-on experience in the IT sector with a proven track record in infrastructure setup and recovery, having served both residential and commercial sectors in Florida after Hurricane Michael. Soon after, I received experience in the Biomedical Engineering field and team leadership during my time in the United States Air Force. In addition, I specialized in the maintenance and calibration of Total Organic Carbon water analysis instruments and gained more team leadership training while at Suez. Notably, I have many years of volunteering for multiple food drives, clean-up crews, and working the concession stands at Mile High Stadium.

In 2022, I pivoted to Data Analytics after having worked in Engineering and IT roles for five years. Making a jump to becoming a Data Analyst was an easy next step for my career. In my previous positions, I often had to analyze data to fix problems, such as the Total Organic Carbon Analyzers at Suez. Because of this, and catered with my own personal interest in analyzing data, making the switch to a certified Data Analyst was perfect for me.

Currently working on freelance projects focused on using Python and R, while diving into Deep Machine Learning and Data Protection in my free time.
